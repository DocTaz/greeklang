Morphological analyses

Every morphological analysis identifies a part of speech. Depending on the part of speech, the analysis identifies further properties.

For conjugated verbs: person, number, tense, mood and voice.

For all substantives: gender, case and number. For adjectives, further identifies degree.

For participial forms of verbs, the "mood" is (untraditionally) considered "participle", and the analysis further identifies tense, voice, gender, case and number.
